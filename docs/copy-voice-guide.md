# SayIt Cross-Site Voice Guide

Use this guide on every page to keep copy human, premium, and easy to trust.

## Brand Voice
- Clear over clever.
- Confident, never hypey.
- Premium but plainspoken.
- Focus on customer outcomes, not agency process.
- Write like a smart small-business owner is reading on their phone.

## Headline Patterns
Use short, direct headlines (3-8 words).

Preferred patterns:
- `Outcome + audience`: `Websites That Help You Get More Calls`
- `Offer + speed`: `Launch Fast. Grow Monthly.`
- `Category + benefit`: `Print Services, Delivered to Your Door`
- `Simple promise`: `Clear Pricing. No Surprises.`

Rules:
- One idea per headline.
- Avoid stacked clauses and buzzwords.
- Never lead with internal model language (example: recurring revenue model).

## CTA Patterns
Primary CTA should feel safe and easy to click.

Primary CTA examples:
- `Get Started`
- `Get My Website Started`
- `See Packages`
- `Get My Recommendation`

Secondary CTA examples:
- `Talk to Us`
- `Call or Text 520-222-6308`
- `View Pricing`

Rules:
- Use action + benefit tone, not pressure.
- Keep labels 2-5 words when possible.
- Put payment language on pricing/checkout steps, not hero buttons.

## Section Naming System
Use consistent section names across pages.

Core section labels:
- `Website Services`
- `Monthly Plans`
- `Print Services`
- `How It Works`
- `Pricing`
- `Portfolio`
- `FAQ`
- `Contact`

Support section labels:
- `Why Businesses Choose SayIt`
- `Recent Work`
- `Simple Promise`
- `Not Ready Yet?`

Rules:
- Keep names literal and scannable.
- Avoid internal terms like pipeline, fulfillment model, conversion architecture.

## Forbidden Jargon (Do Not Use)
Remove internal and over-technical language from customer-facing pages.

Do not use:
- `white label`, `whitelabel`, `drop shipping`, `dropship`
- `recurring revenue model`, `upsell flow`, `attach rate`
- `primary buyer path`, `checkout flow optimization`
- `lead scoring`, `funnel stage`, `pipeline state`
- `one-time + recurring model`
- `operations layer`, `automation architecture`
- `AEO/GEO strategy` (customer copy)
- `technical stack`, `webhook`, `schema markup` (customer copy)

Use this instead:
- `delivered to your door`
- `monthly support`
- `clear next steps`
- `simple process`
- `help you get more calls and leads`

## Copy QA Checklist (Before Publish)
1. Can a non-technical owner understand every section in one pass?
2. Does the page lead with a customer outcome in the first screen?
3. Is there one clear primary CTA on the page?
4. Are payment details shown only where purchase decisions happen?
5. Is every line free of internal jargon?
6. Does the page sound like one consistent voice?
